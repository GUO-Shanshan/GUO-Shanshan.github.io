Tips I Need to Know

Foreign visitors typically need a visa to enter China. Ensure you apply for the appropriate type of visa (e.g., tourist, business) well in advance of your travel dates.

Learn More

The official currency in China is the Renminbi (RMB), also known as the yuan (¥). It's recommended to have some cash for small purchases, although mobile payments like Alipay and WeChat Pay are widely used.

  • Digital Payment: Alipay, WeChat Pay (or Weixin in Chinese)
    • These are the two most popular mobile payment methods in China.
    • They work through apps that allow users to link their bank accounts and make payments by scanning QR codes.
    • They are accepted nearly everywhere—from street vendors to large department stores.
    • Foreign visitors can now use Alipay and WeChat Pay by linking their international credit or debit cards.
    • Alipay:
      • Alipay is a payment platform similar to PayPal, but much more versatile.
      • It allows users to pay for almost anything, from online shopping to in-store purchases.
      • Alipay also integrates a wide range of services, including many "mini programs" from Alibaba's ecosystem, enabling you to book tickets, hail a taxi, pay utility bills, and much more—all within one app.
    • WeChat Pay (Weixin Pay):
      • WeChat Pay is part of WeChat, which is a very popular messaging and social media app in China, similar to WhatsApp or Telegram.
      • Besides messaging, WeChat has many other features, including payment services.
      • WeChat Pay is embedded directly into the WeChat app, allowing users to send money to friends, pay for goods and services, and even order food or book a ride—all in a single, user-friendly app.
  • UnionPay
    • UnionPay is China’s domestic card network, similar to Visa or Mastercard.
    • It is accepted almost universally within China.
    • Many ATMs also support UnionPay, making it easy for visitors to withdraw cash.
    • Foreign cards with the UnionPay logo are also commonly accepted.
  • International Mobile Payment: Apple Pay, Samsung Pay
    • Apple Pay and Samsung Pay can be used in some parts of China, mostly in international chains and major retailers.
    • However, they are not as widely accepted as Alipay or WeChat Pay, so it’s best to check with the specific store.
    • They work just like Alipay and WeChat Pay but rely on NFC terminals instead of QR codes.
  • Credit Cards
    • Major credit cards like Visa, Mastercard, and American Express are accepted in high-end hotels, major restaurants, and large shopping malls.
    • However, small vendors and rural areas may not accept them, so it’s wise to carry some cash or have access to a digital payment method.
    • Note that sometimes, international credit cards can incur a service fee.

Mandarin Chinese is the official language. English is spoken in major cities, but having a translation app or phrasebook can be very helpful in more remote areas.

China is a vast country with diverse climates. Check the weather for your destination and pack accordingly. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters in the north are freezing.

China has an extensive public transportation network, including high-speed trains, subways, and buses. Ride-hailing apps like Didi are widely used for local travel.

Train Travel Guide

Tap water is not safe to drink in most parts of China. Bottled water is widely available. Ensure you have travel insurance and check for any required vaccinations before your trip.
